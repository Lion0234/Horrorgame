# Game Design Overview

**Status:** High-level concept / pre-production direction  
**Last updated:** 2026-07-10  
**Purpose:** Describe the intended finished-game experience, not an implementation plan or locked feature list.

## Vision

HorrorGem is a campaign-first supernatural investigation and exorcism horror game. Hunters enter hostile, mythology-infused places, collect evidence under pressure, identify what they are facing, and turn that knowledge into the means to survive and banish it.

The central feeling is a cycle of dread, discovery, temporary relief, and a more dangerous return. Investigation should create visceral scares and sustained tension; an exorcism should feel like a desperate, life-or-death climax rather than a routine combat encounter.

## Campaign Structure

The campaign is made of acts. Each act centers on one entity category: a coherent family of related creatures drawn from a mythology or folklore tradition. A category contains several lesser "critter-tier" entities and a boss-tier entity from the same family. A draugr and zombie, for example, might be treated as expressions of one category rather than unrelated enemies.

Players first learn the category through critter encounters. Each successful investigation and exorcism produces a token. A witchdoctor interprets these tokens, explaining how they connect to the category's boss and revealing part of the knowledge needed to confront it. Knowledge is therefore both story progression and practical preparation.

The exact encounter count is intentionally open. A reference model is three critter tokens revealing three of the four pieces of information needed to challenge a boss-tier entity.

## Core Act Loop

1. Find a critter-tier entity and enter its biome.
2. Investigate its behavior, environment, and evidence while surviving scares and escalating danger.
3. Identify the entity or its category, then escape to regroup.
4. Use the acquired knowledge and local exorcism tools to confront and banish it.
5. Bring its token to the witchdoctor and learn how it advances the boss investigation.
6. Repeat with further critters, then pursue the boss-tier entity once enough knowledge has been earned.

Boss confrontations may take the form of a chase, battle, trap, containment ritual, or another high-stakes exorcism. The specific form should serve the category rather than follow one fixed formula.

## Investigation, Exorcism, and Biomes

Investigation tools gather and interpret evidence. Exorcism tools are distinct: they should be category-specific and commonly discovered in the entity's biome, making the environment part of the solution rather than only a backdrop.

Each category needs a recognizable biome style, entity rules, evidence language, and exorcism logic. Maps should be unstable, interactive horror spaces: rooms may transform, paths may shift, and hunters may be pulled into an alternate dimension that they must navigate to escape. These environmental disruptions should intensify uncertainty and create memorable set pieces, not merely obstruct progress.

## Future Mode Direction

The campaign and story mode is the immediate focus. A later random-investigation mode may offer self-contained, Phasmophobia-style missions without campaign progression: a map contains the information and tools required to identify and exorcise a randomly selected critter or boss-tier entity.

## Deliberately Unresolved

- The entity-category roster, their boss entities, and the mythology represented by each act.
- The exact evidence types, tool inventory, and exorcism rituals.
- Whether campaign investigations support solo play, co-op, or both.
- The precise number and ordering of critter encounters per act.
- The witchdoctor's narrative role, relationship to the hunters, and limits on their guidance.
